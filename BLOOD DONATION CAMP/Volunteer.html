Guidelines for Volunteers

  1. Before the Event:
    • Attend Training Sessions: Volunteers should attend all training sessions conducted by the camp organizers to understand their roles.
    • Understand the Process: Be familiar with the blood donation process, donor requirements, and emergency protocols.
    • Setup Assistance: Help with setting up donation stations, registration desks, and refreshment areas.
  2. During the Event:
    • Registration & Greeting:
      • Welcome donors with a friendly attitude.
      • Ensure donors complete all necessary forms and health questionnaires.
      • Direct donors to the appropriate donation stations and provide any needed information.
    • Donor Support:
      • Guide donors to the health check-up area for screening.
      • Provide assistance to donors who may feel anxious or nervous.
      • Observe donors after donation for any signs of discomfort or fainting.
      • Ensure donors are resting adequately and consuming refreshments post-donation.
    • Hygiene and Safety Protocols:
      • Ensure that all equipment is sanitized, and that the environment is clean.
      • Volunteers should wear gloves and masks as required.
      • Maintain social distancing in waiting areas, if necessary.
    • Crowd Management:
      • Manage the flow of donors and ensure that the waiting areas are not overcrowded.
      • Direct donors to proper areas and assist with keeping the environment orderly.
  3. Post-Donation Care:
    • Encourage Donor Rest: Encourage donors to stay for at least 10-15 minutes in the recovery area.
    • Provide Refreshments: Offer water, juices, and light snacks to donors.
    • Monitor Donor Well-Being: Check if donors feel well before allowing them to leave.
  4. Post-Event Duties:
    • Cleanup: Help with cleaning up the venue and ensuring all equipment is safely packed away.
    • Documentation: Ensure that all forms and records are complete and submitted to the organizers.
    • Feedback: Collect feedback from donors on their experience, if required.
